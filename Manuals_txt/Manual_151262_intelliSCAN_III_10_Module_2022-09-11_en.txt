Intended Use
The intelliSCAN III 10 Module is an OEM version
which is intended for an integration into a
system with a proper housing.
The scan module is designed for applications
that demand a two dimensional positioning of
a laser beam on a working plane. The
wavelength of the input rays should correspond
to the layout of the scan module. The maximum
rating for the input aperture and laser power
should not be exceeded. Follow the specifica-
tions for the input signal and power supply. If
your application requires different specifica-
tions, please contact SCANLAB.

the following environmental conditions are
met: 
> The storage temperature for the scan
module should be between – 35 °C and
+60 °C.
> The operating temperature is 25 °C ± 10 °C.
> The environment should not be damp or
corrosive.

2.3.2Safety Procedures that the 
Customer is Responsible for
The scan module is usually operated in conjunction
with a laser. Therefore, before installation and oper-
ation, all regulations for safe operation of lasers must
be known and applied. Since SCANLAB has no influ-
ence over the employed laser, the customer is solely
responsible for safe operation of the laser together
with the scan module.
Shutter
The scan module has no shutter and there is no
device to decrease the laser output power. It is the
responsibility of the customer to include such a
device in the system in a way as to comply with all
regulations. The observance of laser safety for the
entire assembly of the system must be ensured. 
Maintenance
During maintenance of the laser equipment, the class
of the laser can increase. Therefore, the customer
must take suitable protective measures. 
Warning symbols
According to VBG 93 and VBG 125 a warning symbol
indicating the class of the employed laser must be
put up such that the area is marked where the
emerging beam is harmful. In addition, a warning
symbol must be placed at the emitting aperture of
the laser system. The table on page 9 shows the
appropriate warning symbols for the various laser
classes according to VBG 93.
Danger!
• The operation of the scan module must
comply with all applicable safety regula-
tions.
• Safety regulations may differ from coun-
try to country. It is the responsibility of the
customer to comply with all local regula-
tions.
!

The intelliSCAN III 10 Module – Principle of 
Operation
The scan module deflects a parallel beam of rays
in XY direction. Figure 2 shows the principle of
operation. The beam enters the scan module
through the input aperture. Once inside, the
beam hits mirror 1 on galvanometer scanner 1,
then it is deflected by mirror 2 on galvanometer
scanner 2. The deflection angle is determined
by the adjustable position of the galvanometer
scanner. The beam leaves the scan module
through the output opening that can carry an
F-Theta objective.
The F-Theta objective focuses the parallel beam
of rays on the working plane. With the F-Theta
objective, the angle of incidence of the beam
and the position of the focal point on the image
field are directly proportional.
The pillow-barrel-shaped distortion of the
square image field is caused by the path of the
beam in the scan module and by the objective.
This field distortion must be compensated by
the control. See section 5.3 "Reference System
for the Input Signal", page 28.
The scan module is designed for a parallel beam
of rays with a defined maximum diameter that
is marked as aperture. A beam within this
device should not exceed this diameter.
3.1
Deflection Mirrors
The deflection mirrors are intended for a
specific beam diameter, called aperture. This
diameter affects the characteristics of the
optical image. The way the deflection mirrors
are designed for a specific aperture affects the
dynamic behaviour of the scan module. The
aperture of the deflection mirrors is 10 mm. The
use of beams that have a larger diameter inside
the scan module might result in damages.
The deflection mirrors have a dielectrical high
performance coating: The coating is designed
for a defined wavelength. 
• The mirrors are designed for a wavelength of 
355 nm.
• At a wavelength of 355 nm, the maximum

3.2
Objective 
The objective has to have the matching focal length
and be set up for the used wavelength. The distance
A between the laser input and the image field
(figure 3) and the size of the image field depend on
various factors, e.g. the focal length of the objective
and the aperture of the scan module. Furthermore,
the distance A depends on the divergence of the
input beam.
Due to the mechanical dimensions of the mirrors in
the scan module, some vignetting of the beam can
occur, if the scan angle exceeds a certain value. That
means, a part of the beam can not any more be lead
by the mirrors. This part of the beam is absorbed by
the scan module resulting in a loss of power.
Furthermore the interior of the scan module might be
damaged due to the absorption of laser radiation.
Using a gaussian beam profile, the vignetting of the
laser beam is insignificant, if the scan angle of each
mirror does not exceed the size of the "maximum
scan angle" 
defined in chapter 10 "Technical Specifications
intelliSCAN III 10 Module".
Caution!
• Using scan angles exceeding the size of
the maximum scan angle indicated in
chapter 10, some vignetting inside the
scan module can occur and the interior of
the scan module might be damaged.
If your application requires other scan
angles, please contact SCANLAB.

4.3.3Data Cable Guidelines
Signal transfer between scan module and controller
is realized via a data cable. This data cable is
connected to SL2-100 connector.
The differential DATA IN input channel receives the
control values from the controller. The differential
DATA OUT output channel returns the status signals
to the controller. The 3.3 V voltage is supplied for
SCANLAB’s POF converter for optical data trans-
mission (POF = Polymer Optical Fiber) – also see
below. This voltage should not be used for other
purposes.
Appropriate data cables, for either electrical trans-
mission or optical fiber transmission, are obtainable
from SCANLAB. The optical fiber, too, is attached via
a 9-pin D-SUB connector. Optical conversion (POF
conversion) for optical data transmission takes place
in the D-SUB connector. The operating voltage for
POF conversion is supplied at the SL2-100 connector
and the RTC5 board’s scan head connectors.
Data cables are generally not included in the
package. SCANLAB recommends the following
design (for electrical data transmission):
• The cable should be fitted with 9-pin male D-SUB
connectors at both ends. The two channels DATA
IN± and DATA OUT± (4 wires) must consist of
twisted cable pairs and be cross-connected at
both D-SUB connectors (e.g. so that the RTC5
board’s DATA OUT signal flows to the scan
system’s DATA IN input). The cable length should
not exceed 25 m. SCANLAB recommends a cable
impedance of 110 W, independent from the cable
length.
• The data cable must have coaxial copper braided
shielding.
• The D-SUB connectors must have fully shielded
metal housings.
• The electrical connection of the cable’s braided
shielding to the D-SUB housing should not be
implemented as a wire. Instead, the cable’s
braided shielding should be coaxially connected to
the D-SUB housing via shielded clamps.
• The data cable’s controller end must be fitted with
a ferrit ring (e.g. Würth WE 742 711 32).
Figure 14 shows the data cable layout and pin assign-
ments.
• Connect the scan module via the data cable to the
customer-specific controller.
• If an RTC board is used as the controller, then
follow the installation instructions in the RTC
manual.

5.4
Assuring Safe Operating 
Temperatures
If the scanners are driven for long periods of time at
high positioning speeds or if the application includes
a high rate of vector changes, the correspondingly
high current consumption of the galvanometer
scanners can lead to excessive temperatures –
especially in the case of insufficient cooling, for

Stage 1: Temperature Status Warning
The temperature status signal TempOK indicates that
the scanner is operating at a safe temperature level.
During normal operation, the signal is 1. 
If the scanner temperature rises above a certain value
or drops below a minimum value, then the TempOK
signal switches to 0. If the TEMPOK signal switches to
0 during operation, system operation should be
stopped and the system should be checked to
determine the cause. If system operation is not
stopped, large drift or other side-effects may occur.
Stage 2: Critical Temperature Shutdown
In addition to the temperature status warning, the
following scanner protective function is imple-
mented:
If a scanner’s temperature rises above the critical
value for temperature status warning and reaches a
second, still higher critical value, then the scanner’s
output stage is turned off to prevent damage to the
scanner. In this situation, the scanner’s position is
stationary and no longer under programmatic
control.
If the scanner’s temperature drops again below the
power-down threshold, the scanner’s drive stage is
automatically restarted and the scan module will
resume normal operation.

output stage will be shut down. In such situations,
the galvanometer scanner can no longer be

6.1.1Regular Cleaning of the

Dirty objectives and mirrors increase the

> Regularly check all objectives and mirrors. In

> Carefully hold the optical element by its

(electronic or spectrometer cleaner).
> Put the dampened end of the tissue on one
end of the optics and slowly pull it across. Do
not rub back and forth!
> If necessary, remove the rest of the Ethanol
with the dry end of the tissue.
> Repeat the above until the optical surface is
completely clean. For every cleaning swipe,
use a new objective cleaning tissue.
