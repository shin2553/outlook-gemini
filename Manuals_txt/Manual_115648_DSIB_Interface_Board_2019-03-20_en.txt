DSC Digital Servo Control
 
1
Overview
The DSC electronics enables together with an RTC PC 
interface board (RTC4 or higher) a full digital control 
of scan systems. 
The DSC electronics consists of:
• two DSCB boards with a digital controller each for 
one galvanometer scanner/scan axis
• a DSIB digital interface board which serves as 
interface for digital data transfer between a RTC 
board and the both DSCB boards
The arrangement and cabling of the boards is shown 
in figure 1.
The DSCB digital control board forms a closed servo 
loop in conjunction with the galvanometer scanner. 
Each DSCB board is individually tuned by SCANLAB 
for its assigned scanner-mirror configuration. Do not 
interchange the control boards. Optimum scan qual-
ity cannot be guaranteed if control boards are inter-
changed. Therefore, make sure the various electronic 
components are properly combined. To help achieve 
this, the connectors and each DSCB board is marked 
with “Galvo 1” or “Galvo 2”. Additionally, the serial 
number is marked on the angle plate of each DSCB 
board.

2
Installation
2.1
DSCB Digital Control Board
Figure 2 shows the dimensions of the DSCB digital 
control board with its connectors. The board is 
mounted on an angle plate. For fastening the control 
board, the angle plate’s side surface provides four 
bore holes. Two of the four holes are M3-threaded. 
All holes require M3-threaded screws. Ensure that no 
electrical contact exists between the screws and the 
board’s electronic components.
To prevent damage or destruction of the board from 
excessive heat, it is important to attach the board’s 
angle plate to a heat sink or an appropriate-sized 
part of a housing. The material and dimension of the 
heat sink must be chosen such that the temperature 
of the angle plate does not exceed 50 °C. 
Caution!
• Store the electronics boards always in an elec-
trostatically neutral environment in the deliv-
ered antistatic bag.
• To avoid damage to the electronics boards by 
static charges, observe all applicable ESD 
guidelines.
• Don’t touch the contacts of the electronics 
boards.
• The electronics boards must be securely 
fastened and protected from moisture, dust, 
corrosive vapors and external influences.

2.2
DSIB Interface Board
Figure 3 shows the dimensions of the DSIB board 
with its connectors. The board is mounted on an 
angle plate.
For fastening the board, the angle plate’s side sur-
face provides four bore holes. Two of the four holes 
are M3-threaded. All holes require M3- threaded 
screws. Ensure that no electrical contact exists 
between the screws and the board’s electronic com-
ponents.
The DSIB board must be mounted so that the 
included flat ribbon cables can be connected to the 
DSIB board and the DSCB board.

3
Connections
3.1
Power Supply
Power input to the scan module is via the 2-pin 
POWER IN connector on the DSIB board. Figure 4 
shows the pinout of this connector.
 
The board requires a power supply of +30 V DC 
(±1 V). The maximum current depends on the con-
nected galvo and is at least 1.5 A.
For mating plugs, connector housings of type JST 
VHR-2N and crimp contacts of type JST SVH-41T-P1.1 
(cable diameter AWG20-16) are suitable.
3.2
Data Transfer
For connecting the DSC electronics to the RTC board, 
the DIGITAL IN connector of the DSIB board is used. 
Figure 5 shows the pinout of this connector. 
The control and the scan kit are connected with a 
serial interface. The data transmission between the 
signal input and signal output follows the XY2-100 
protocol. Three signals are transmitted:
• The control delivers the set value of the X axis 
(scanner 2) and Y axis (scanner 1).
• The scan kit generates a status signal.
• Two other channels transmit the synchronization 
for data transport and a clock signal.
SCANLAB recommends to use a data cable length of 
max. 10 m.
 
3.3
Cabling
The cabling of the galvanometer scanners, boards 
and the PC controller is shown in figure 1. Proceed as 
follows:
• Connect each galvanometer scanner via the two 
mounted cables to the associated DSCB board.
• Connect the DIGITAL & POWER IN and POWER IN 
connectors of the DSCB boards via four suitable 
cables to the associated DIGITAL & POWER OUT 
and POWER OUT connectors of the DSIB board.
• Connect the PC controller with RTC interface 
board via a suitable cable to the DIGITAL IN 
connector of the DSIB board.
• Connect the power supply to the POWER IN 
connector of the DSIB board.
